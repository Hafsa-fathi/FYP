Research Abstract

Sri Lanka is a small Island with an enormous population. Its increasingly creating economy further makes land a rewarding wellspring of fixed capital. On the other hand, land titling is a complex and lengthy process, where different government bodies process various arrangements of reports, and administrative provisos energize fake activities by coordinated people. Therefore, the ongoing model experiences great administration.

In this project, we propose a Blockchain-based solution that offers information synchronization and transparency, ease of access, immutable records management, a quicker and less expensive arrangement. Considering the innovative information and capacity of the people and the government, we presented a stage by stage Blockchain adoption model that begins with a public Blockchain record.

We give detailed smart contracts plans of the public Blockchain and execute a model framework utilizing Ethereum. Our exploratory arrangement utilizes local blockchain. As an outcome, Blockchain adoption can further develop the land title digitization effort of Sri Lanka.

Our Goal

The main objective of our project is to develop a decentralized application using Blockchain which could overcome the short-comings of the existing process of land registry in Sri Lanka. And maintains all records safely and also up to date a certain record with better transparency, high security, true traceability, and cost-effective manner based on its immutable characteristics.
